Airplanes - Powered Aircraft

An airplane or aeroplane is a powered, fixed-wing aircraft that is propelled forward by thrust from a jet engine, propeller or rocket engine.

Usage:

The broad spectrum of uses for airplanes includes recreation, transportation of goods and people, military, and research.

Parts:

Engines:

The engines are the cylindrical parts usually found under (or sometimes just above) the wings, and they drive the plane forward. the goal is the same: to push air behind the plane and force the plane forward.

Tails:

The tail is the very back of the plane. Most of the tail is stationary, but it also contains movable parts called flaps. There is usually a vertical flap called a rudder, which points the plane left or right. here are also two horizontal flaps called elevators, which point the plane up or down. Together, these are what the pilot uses to control the plane.

Wings:

The wings of the plane are what make it able to fly in the first place--they produce an upward force called lift. The wings are the parts that stretch out from the side of the plane. Wings can stretch out perfectly straight or be angled up slightly because an upward angle reduces the chance of the plane rolling unexpectedly.

Fuselage:

The fuselage is the middle of the plane, where humans and cargo are found. The fuselage is a part that doesn't really aid in the flight--its purpose is to hold the passengers and cargo.

Cockpit:

Is where the pilot and co-pilot sit to control the plane. A modern cockpit is a confusing array of screens, lights, dials, and buttons doing all kinds of things. Let's take a quick look at the main parts of a cockpit.

Landing Gear:

The landing gear is the principal support of the airplane when parked, taxiing, taking off, or landing. The most common type of landing gear consists of wheels, but airplanes can also be equipped with floats for water operations or skis for landing on snow.

Seat Capacity:

Commercial Planes :- ranging from 150 to about 200.

Private Jets :- ranging from 10 to 19.

Private Propellor Planes :- Around 10.

Average Cost:

Single-Engine :- $15,000 - $100,000.

Multi-Engine :- $75,000 - $300,000.
